HFEL, KAIST

2024 — 2025

CV-based monocular 3D pose estimation for ergonomic risk assessment

When is an off-the-shelf pose model accurate enough to score ergonomic risk — and when does kinematic error change the decision?

  • Compared MediaPipe and MMPose against an 8-camera OptiTrack reference across three camera views (front, 45°, side) and a range of static and dynamic tasks.
  • Separated kinematic fidelity (joint positions and angles) from ergonomic-decision robustness — the degree to which kinematic errors actually change REBA / RULA risk classification.
  • MediaPipe outperformed MMPose overall; 45° was its best view; intraclass correlations of 0.87 (REBA) and 0.92 (RULA) against the reference.
  • Authors: Aghazadeh & Xiong. Submitted to Ergonomics, revision in progress.

Paper 2 · in preparation

HFEL, KAIST

2024 — 2025

ErgoYar — a mobile ergonomic-assessment app for non-experts

Translating a validated CV pipeline into a mobile tool an SME worker or supervisor can run without training.

  • Built a Flutter front-end and FastAPI / Supabase backend that turn a smartphone video into per-frame REBA / RULA scoring with an annotated pose overlay.
  • Designed the interface around progressive disclosure (overview → body part → joint detail) and body-part-specific feedback rather than a single composite score, so the density of per-joint output stays interpretable on a small screen.
  • Pilot usability study with 10 non-expert users: SUS 80.5 (excellent band, ~90th percentile), uMARS > 3.5 across all six dimensions, SUS–uMARS Pearson r = 0.74.
  • All ten participants completed the seven-task protocol without facilitator intervention.

2025 — present

Pediatric Digital Sensor Study (PDSS)

Building the digital-phenotyping pipeline that turns raw sensor recordings from 103 children into a dataset clinicians and researchers can analyze.

  • Designed and built the per-(child × task) feature pipeline: four sensor modalities (eye-tracking, keyboard, stylus, voice), ~30 tasks per child, 14,500+ raw files, ~1,500 features per row.
  • Chose session-level windowing over fixed 15s / 60s windows after a quality-check pass showed the latter would drop roughly half the tasks; this preserved 100% task coverage across modalities.
  • Diagnosed and patched three blocking bugs in a partner lab's feature extractor without modifying their code, using in-memory wrappers — kept their version control intact and made every patch explicit.
  • Trained K-SADS ADHD models on the resulting features: inattention classification reached AUC 0.72 (LogReg on VST core features); hyperactivity classification reached F2 0.65 (LogReg on AST + VST + Flanker core); hyperactivity severity regression reached Pearson r = 0.54 (ElasticNet on aggregate features).
  • Working alongside our project manager on product-side tasks — scoping, sizing, and writing up decisions — to learn the part of the work that lives upstream of the code.
