**Konglish** (콩글리시) is Korean's playful borrowing of English words — pronounced and reshaped to fit Korean phonology, sometimes used in ways the original English doesn't.

Examples that catch foreigners off guard:

- **핸드폰** (haendeupon) — "hand phone" = mobile phone. Standard usage everywhere.
- **아이쇼핑** (aishyoping) — "eye shopping" = window shopping.
- **원샷** (wonshyat) — "one shot" = bottoms-up, drink it all.
- **포카리스웨트** (pokariseuweteu) — Pocari Sweat — beloved sports drink, name unbothered by English connotations.
- **셀카** (selka) — "self camera" = selfie.

Why it's worth paying attention to:
1. You'll hear it constantly in conversation.
2. It often *looks* like English but means something different — false friends are common.
3. It's a window into how Korean and English are negotiating coexistence in everyday speech.
